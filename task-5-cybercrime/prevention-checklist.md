# Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students

Navigating the digital world on campus requires vigilance. Use this checklist to protect your personal data, academic work, and finances.

### Financial & UPI Safety
1. **Never enter a PIN to receive money:** Remember that your UPI PIN is only required to *send* money or check your balance. If a prompt asks for your PIN to receive a payment or refund, it is a scam.
2. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for transactions:** Campus or cafe Wi-Fi networks are often unsecured. Always switch to mobile data or use a trusted VPN before making UPI payments or accessing your bank accounts. 

### Device & Account Security
3. **Download tools and mods carefully:** Whether you are installing new environments for PyCharm or adding mods to games on Steam or Epic Games, only download from official, verified sources to avoid hidden malware or keyloggers.
4. **Scrutinize shared study materials:** Be cautious with files shared in large college WhatsApp or Telegram groups. Cybercriminals often disguise malicious payloads as "Exam_Solutions.pdf" or "Project_Presentation.ppt".
5. **Lock down your primary accounts:** Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your main email, social media, and academic portals. This creates a critical second barrier even if your password is compromised.
6. **Practice safe shared-computer habits:** When using computers in the college lab or library, use Incognito/Private browsing mode. Always log out manually and never allow the browser to save your passwords.
7. **Keep software updated:** Regularly update your operating system, web browsers, and antivirus software. These updates often contain critical patches for newly discovered security vulnerabilities.
8. **Verify urgent requests:** If a friend or family member sends a sudden message asking for emergency funds via UPI, do not send the money immediately. Call them directly on a known phone number to verify their identity and the situation.

---

### Emergency Reporting Channels
If you or someone you know falls victim to cybercrime, report it immediately to the authorities in India:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** Visit [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) to file a complaint online.
* **National Helpline Number:** Dial **1930** immediately to report financial fraud and potentially freeze the unauthorized transaction.
