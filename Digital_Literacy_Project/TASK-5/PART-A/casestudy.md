### Case Study: Ransomware Attack on "MediCare Plus" Clinic

**The Crime:** Ransomware is a severe type of malicious software that encrypts a victim's files or entirely locks them out of their network. The cybercriminals then demand a ransom payment—almost always in untraceable cryptocurrency like Bitcoin—in exchange for the decryption key needed to restore access.

**The Scenario (Realistic Fiction):** MediCare Plus, a regional health clinic, relies entirely on digital patient records. One morning, the staff discovered they could not access any files. Instead of their usual login portals, a red screen appeared demanding $50,000 in Bitcoin within 48 hours, threatening to delete the encryption key if the payment was delayed.

**How It Happened (Step-by-Step):**
1. **Initial Breach:** An administrator received an email appearing to be from a trusted medical supplier, containing an urgent "Overdue Invoice" attachment.
2. **Execution:** The employee downloaded and opened the disguised file, which secretly executed a malicious payload in the background.
3. **Encryption:** The ransomware silently propagated across the clinic's internal network, encrypting databases, patient histories, and even the local backup drives connected to the main server.
